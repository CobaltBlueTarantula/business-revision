## Profits

Most businesses exist to **make a profit** and provide a return to the owners as a **reward for their entrepreneurship**. In order to maximise profit, managers must implement strategies that **maximise revenue** and **minimise costs**.

!!Income statements!! (**revenue statements**) are used to determine how profitable the business is.

## Market share

Market share is the **percentage of total sales** a business has compared with its **competitors in a particular market**. Increased market share leads to higher sales and revenue, and therefore profit.

## Growth

Growth may be related to profitability, but also various other areas:

- capacity of size
- horizontal integration and takeovers
- vbertical integration of suppliers
- diversification through acquisition
- exporting products or investing overseas
- increasing the value of assets
- opening more outlets or franchises

## Share price

The price of a share in financial markets is an indication of the **total value of the business**.

Maintaining a high share price makes a business **less vulnerable to takeovers** by making controlling shares more expensive. A business can sustain its share price by:

- being well managed
- earning consistent profits
- controlling debt levels
- providing worthwhile returns to shareholders

## Social

Businesses have a responsibility to people and society, as their activities can have an **impact on quality of life**. A **positive perception by customers** and the community can significantly **influence business success**.

A business that acts in a **socially responsible manner** will receive long-term support from society. This will aid in achieving **market share and profits**.

## Environmental

A business that incorporates **ecologically sustainable practices** may promote this as part of its marketing plan in order to **improve its reputation**.
