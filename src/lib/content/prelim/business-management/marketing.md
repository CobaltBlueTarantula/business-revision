## Identification of the target market

**Target market:** the focus of the firm's marketing strategy, including existing as well as potential customers.<br/>
**Market segmentation:** the division of the total market into small segments based on common characteristics of a customer group. The largest market is the **mass market** and the smallest is a **niche market**.<br/>
**Market research:** analysis of data from a target market group to create a customer profile.

## Marketing mix (PPPP)

### Product

**Positioning:** the image of the quality of a product in the mind of the consumer compared with alternative products offered by competitors.<br/>
**Brand:** a name, design, or symbol that distinguishes a product from the produuct of another business.

### Price

**Cost-of-production:** a profit margin or mark-up added to the production cost.<br/>
**Demand pricing:** pricing is based on what consumers are willing to pay.<br/>
**Competition-based pricing:** Competitors' prices are used as a guide.

### Placement

How a product is distributed to the market, including:

- Transportation
- Storage
- Outlets and Locations

**Transport logistics:** physical flow of goods / services.<br/>
**Distribution channels:** how the product is moved from the place of manufacture to the consumer.

### Promotion (SAPP)

**Sales promotion:** incentives to the consumer through competitions or offers.<br/>
**Advertising:** advertisements in newspapers, magazines, billboard, television or online.<br/>
**Personal selling:** direct phone calls, emails, text messages, and in-person selling.<br/>
**Publicity:** public featuring of the business's name and logo in a positive light, such as on a program.
