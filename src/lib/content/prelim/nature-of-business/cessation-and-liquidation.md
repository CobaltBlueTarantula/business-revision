## Voluntary Cessation

The business owners choose to **close the business themselves**. Owners wind up operations in an orderly way, **pay remaining debts**, and distribute any leftover assets. **No legal insolvency process** is required.

## Involuntary Cessation

The business is **forced to close**, usually by **creditors** or a **court order**, because it **cannot pay its debts**.

## Liquidation

The process of converting a company's assets into **cash to pay creditors**. An **independent liquidator is appointed** to sell off all assets, pay debts in a legally defined order of priority, and then dissolve the company. Can be either voluntary (**creditors' voluntary liquidation**) or involuntary (**court-ordered**). The company ceases to exist at the end.

## Receivership

A secured creditor (typically a bank) **appoints a receiver** to take control of **specific assets that were used as security for a loan**. The receiver's job is to **recover the money owed to that one creditor** (not necessarily to act in the interest of all creditors or the business). The company may continue trading or be wound up depending on circumstances.

## Administration

A temporary process where an **independent administrator** takes control of an **insolvent company** to assess its future. The goal is to **find the best outcome for creditors**, whether it is to restructure and **save the business**, or **liquidation** if no viable path exists.

## Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy applies only to **sole traders and partnerships**. It is a legal process where a **person who cannot pay their debts** has their financial affairs handled by a trustee. Their **assets are used to pay creditors**, and they face restrictions. Companies go into liquidation instead of bankruptcy.
