## Establishment

- Generating sufficient revenue to cover costs and provide a profit to the owner
- Establishing a customer base and marketing strategies
- Accessing finance without proven history or reputation
- Ensuring all government regulations are followed

## Growth

- Developing effective stock control methods (eg. JIT method)
- Managing rapid expansion which creates pressure on existing structure
- Handling all functions of the business, requiring outsourcing

## Maturity

- Maintaining customer loayalty and interest in products
- Developing new products and innovating to avoid complacency
- Motivating employees and management so tasks don't become boring
- Market saturation makes it difficult to expand within the market

## Post-maturity

- Renewing the business with innovative ideas and products
- Avoiding complacency and decline by being aware of competitive situation
- Aware of legal responsibilities to creditors and suppliers in case of closure
