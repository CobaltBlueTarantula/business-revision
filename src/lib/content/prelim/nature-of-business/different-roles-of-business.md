## Profit

Profit is essential for a business to meet its **day-to-day expenses**.

## Employment

The production of goods and services requires the knowledge, skills and effort of **human resources**.

!!Downsize:!! decrease the scale of operations. For example, through a reduction in the number of staff employed.

## Incomes

Employees are paid an **income by the business**, allowing all parts of the workforce to spend parts of it to **satisfy their needs and wants**.

## Choice

The choice between many brands in a **competitive market environment** encourages businesses to provide goods and services at the **lowest possible price** with the **highest quality**.

## Innovation

Competition encourages businesses to be innovative and **differentiate** themselves in the market. Innovation applies to new products, production process, or marketing improvements. It is crucial for a business to [sustain a competitive advantage](../business-planning/sustaining-competitive-advantage).

## Entrepreneurship and risk

An entrepreneur is an individual that has developed an idea and is willing to take a risk to implement these ideas through a business.

## Wealth

Wealth refers to the **total value of all assets owned** by a person or business.

- By **increasing sales** and **promoting brand awareness** through strategies, management hopes to **increase the value of their organisation**.
- **Higher share price** on the ASX creates **wealth for shareholders** through selling shares at a higher price to earn **dividends**.
- Businesses generate **wealth for the community** because their profits are **taxed by the government**.

## Quality of life

Business **research and development** has contributed to a **significant improvement in our quality of life**.
