A business cannot always sustain above-average performances in all areas for long periods. For this reason, it must choose a strategy that will allow it to use and develop its strengths as a business. This strategy can give the business a !!competitive advantage!!.

## Price/cost strategy

Reducing the price will create a cometitive advantage if the price is **lower** than the price offered by competitiors. However, it will have the effect of reducing the **potential profit**.

Reducing costs is a more effective method, as it will enable a business to lead the way by achieving the **lowest production costs**, allowing it to **reduce product price** while still maintaining a healthy !!profit margin!!.

- !!Efficiency of operations!! refers to the abilitity of a business to **streamline** the processes that occur within that business.
- !!Low-cost labour!! is an obvious way to gain a cost advantage over the competition.
- !!Economies of scale!! can achieve a cost advantage for the firm by **reducing the cost of inputs** and **increasing the level of outputs** (eg. using more machinery, buying in bulk or increasing specialisation of labour).
- !!Decreasing workforce size!! or using machinery where possible can also **reduce the cost of labour**.
- !!Using technology!! can enable businesses to **improve efficiency and production**, resulting in **reduction of overall costs**.

## Differentiation strategy

This strategy focuses on offering customers something that is **not already offered** by business rivals. Otherwise, SMEs will find it very difficult to compete on cost due to the **large budgets** of bigger companies, as well as the **economies of scale** that can be achieved. Therefore, they should adopt a !!differentiation strategy!! and compete on other factors, such as:

- value
- quality
- brand image
- service
- flexibility

Once a business achieves its product differentiations trategy, the challenge then is to **sustain this competitive advantage**, especially as competitors attempt to match it.

## Ensuring long-term success

Long-term success is assured if a business sustains its competitive edge. This involves resisting the actions of competitors who are introducing new strategies, while also **tracking changes within the industry**.

Most importantly, the **actions of management must be dynamic**, as staying in front of competition requires constant action. To sustain a competitive advantage, a manager must be able to **predict changes and trends**, and act on these predictions.

### Limiting the advances of competitors

Businesses must **reduce opporutunities available**, which may be achieved through:

- !!research and development!!, which helps the business **innovate**
- !!patents/copyrights!!, which protect against copying of inventions
- !!exclusive contracts with suppliers!!, which mean a supplier gives **preference** to the business
- !!lobbying government!! to limit foreign competition, which aims to **restrict imported products**
