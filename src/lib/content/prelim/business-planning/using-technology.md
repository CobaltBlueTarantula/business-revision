## Internet applications (e-business and e-commerce)

A business that wants to be **competitive** must adopt the appropriate technology. Businesses **slow to exploit technology** are likely to fail because competitiors will strive to capture **greater market share**. Technology allows operation processes to be more **efficient** and **effective**, **cutting costs** and **improving productivity**.

## Accessing electronic information via the internet

Accessing material electronically is **fast and relatively inexpensive**, while being able to answer many individual requests. However some downsides include hardware, software and service expenses, spam, and information overload.

## E-business

!!E-business:!! using the internet to conduct business. This includes:

- Using the internet to find information
- Using email to communicate with customers and suppliers
- Using the internet to streamline transactions electronically
- Creating an intranet to manage business activities
- Using the internet to trade goods and services online
- Providing online training for staff
- Using the internet to research market conditions, industry tends and economic forecasts
- Using a website to market goods and services
- Using the internet to lodge forms or apply for licences.

## E-commerce

!!E-commerce!! refers to buying and selling goods and services via the internet. Today consumers expect a business to have an **online presence**, however many small business owners still do not realise its full potential.
