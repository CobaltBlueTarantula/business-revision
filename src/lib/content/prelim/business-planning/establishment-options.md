## Setting up a business from scratch

Usually the best option if a person has **created something unique**, recognises a **gap in the market**, or existing businesses **cannot supply all customers**.

### Advantages

- The owner has the **freedom** to set up the business as they wish
- They are able to determine the **pace of growth** and change
- Possible to begin at a **smaller scale** if funds are limited
- There is **no goodwill** to pay for

!!Goodwill:!! the monetary value attached to the reputation of a particular business.

### Disadvantages

- **High risk** and uncertainty
- Difficulty securing finance due to **lack of business reputation**
- **Time is needed** to set up, create procedures, develop a customer base, employ and train staff
- The business may **not generate profits** for some time

## Buying an existing business

The business is already operating and everything associated with it is **included in the purchase**, including stock, equipment, premises, customer base, staff, location, reputation. However it is important to know **why the business is for sale**, as well as examine the **financial health** of the business.

### Advantages

- Sales to existing customers will generate **instant income**
- A good business history increases the **chance of success**
- Proven track record makes it **easier to obtain finance**
- Stock has already been aquired and is **ready for sale**
- The seller may offer **advice and training**
- **Equipment** is available for immediate use
- **Existing employees** can provide assistance.

### Disadvantages

- Existing image and policies may be **difficult to change**, especially in the case of a **poor reputation**
- Success may have been due to the **previous owner's personality and contacts**, which may be lost
- Value of **goodwill** is **difficult to assess** and introduces the chance of overpaying
- Some employees may **resist any change** to the business operation
- There may be **hidden problems**

## Purchasing a franchise

!!Franchise:!! buying the rights from another business to distribute its products under its name.<br/>
!!Franchisor:!! an individual or business that grants a franchise.<br/>
!!Franchisee:!! an individual or business that purchases a franchise.

### Advantages

- Immediate benefits derived from the **franchisor's goodwill** and **established name**
- The franchisor often provides **training and management backup**
- A franchisee can **succeed with limited experience**
- Equipment and premises design are **established and operational**
- Planned advertising already exists
- **Volume buying** results in **cheaper stock**
- A business plan and **proven business methods** already exist
