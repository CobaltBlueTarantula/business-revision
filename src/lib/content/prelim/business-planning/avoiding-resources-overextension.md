Some business owners overextend their capacity in the **establishment or takover stage**, resulting in !!excess expenditure!! that is financed primarilty by **external sources** such as banks.

## Financial overextension

Examples of overextending financially include:

- using **hire purchase** and/or **leasing commitments** made on cars, equipment, office furniture, plant and machinery and premises
- purchasing excess stock
- employing too many staff for the business's current needs

!!Fixed expenditures!! like these create a **higher degree of business risk**, as **weakening economic conditions** can apply greater **financial pressure** and are more susceptible to bankruptcy.

### Avoiding financial overextension

- !!business planning!! involves the completion of **cash flow projections**, preliminary establishment costs, personal financial budgets and financial statements of position, along with the setting of **goals and objectives**.
- !!avoiding an overdependence on debt financing!! helps avoid problems during **weak economic periods** when lenders or creditors are unwilling to wait for the business to overcome difficulties.
- !!long-term financial planning!! concerns both the **current financial aspects** of the business and anticipation of **future problems**, as well as planning the business's **direction** in terms of **growth and expansion**.
- !!starting slow!! is important as it allows the market to determine the **right times for expansion and growth**.

## Overextension of other resources

### Stock

Overextending in terms of stock means investing too much money in **goods or raw materials**. This may occur if the business anticipates customer demand incorrectly without establishing whether it is **saleable among its customers**.

Stock is **unrealised sales**, and a business with too much stock not only **ties up its own cash** but also potentially **loses revenue**. !!Just-in-time!! is a stock control method used by some firms to ensure that **inventory is not lying idle**, and orders are supplied immediately when they're needed.

### Staff

A business may find itself with too many staff, usually due to **overoptimism about sales** or poor job allocations and **hiring**. Businesses can decrease their dependence on staff through:

- outsourcing
- redefining current job descriptions
- using more equipment/machinery
