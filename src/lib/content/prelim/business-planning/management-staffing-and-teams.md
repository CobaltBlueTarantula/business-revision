The role of management is to:

- Make the best use of **limited resources** to achieve its goals
- Work with and through other **people** to achieve goals in a rapidly chaning environment

A **manager's skill** is the most critical factor in determining a business's success of failure.

A skilful manager will have the ability to:

- Solve difficult problems
- Set and help attain important goals
- Create a positive !!business culture!! through their words and actions
- Monitor the business's external and internal environments
- Plan and allocate resources
- Coordinate teams and departments
- Monitor the performance of teams and indidividuals
- Successfully put workplace and business changes into action
- Develop and implement schedules and operating plans

## Staffing

It is essential for success to have employees who are **satisfied and motivated**, as they will be more **productive**. They also provide the business with a competitive advantage, therefore the management of the **staffing function** must be undertaken with care.

SME owners often fail to provide enough **time** or **financial resources** to carry out the recruitment process, often leading to misfortune for both the SME owner and the employee.

Many businesses use !!recruitment agencies!! as a way of decreasing the time involved in searching for a new employee.

!!Skills audit:!! a process that establishes the **current skills levels** of employees and **future skills requirements**.<br/>
!!Skills inventory:!! a database containing information on the **skills, abilities and qualifications** of existing staff.

## Teams

By investing time and money in the !!development and training!! of team leaders and team members, the business reaps the rewards of **improved productivity**.

Businesses are learning that teams:

- make more informed and **creative decisions**
- work without the need for close supervision
- create greater levels of **employee cooperation**
- provide improved customer service and/or **production output**
