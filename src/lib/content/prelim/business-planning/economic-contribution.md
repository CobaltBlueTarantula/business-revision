!!Economy:!! a system used to determine what to produce, how to produce and to whom production will be distributed.

### Gross domestic product (GDP)

!!Gross domestic product (GDP):!! the total money value of all goods and services produces in Australia over a one-year period.

Since SMEs produce a wide range of goods and services, they contribute to over half of Australia's GDP.

### Employment

SMEs make up the **majority of Australian businesses**, therefore they collectively employ a **large proportion of the workforce**, reducing unemployment and increasing household income across the economy.

Other effects include:

- Employment directly improves living standards of employed people
- The state must provide less unemployment benefits
- SMEs and their employees boost government revenue through taxation
- Taxation provides goods and services that benefit the whole community

### Balance of payments (BOP)

!!Balance of payments (BOP):!! a record of a countries trade and financial transactions with the rest of the world over time (usually one year).

The adaptability and flexibility of SMEs makes them more responsive to the needs of overseas markets, leading to them being more successful at exporting than large businesses. This helps maintain a favourable BOP and encourages economic growth by exporting more than is imported.

### Invention and innovation

SMEs are the **main source of most inventions and innovations** in Australia, since they generally display a consistently positive attitude towards it. Finding new ways to do things results in **improved efficiency** and **increased productivity**. However, SMEs often face difficulty obtaining adequate finance when undertaking R&D, since traditional banks perceive it as risky. One possible source of finance in this case is !!venture capital!! from investors that take an **equity position** in the business and **supplement finance**.

## Entering the global market

- !!Working with distributors!! provides a business with **established markets** and a **local presence**, but the business has **minimum control** over the way customers are dealt with or products are marketed.
- A !!joint venture!! with a local business partner also provides the benefits of **local market knowledge and contacts**. This involves an organisation setting up a **business relationship** with a **company in the local market**.
- !!E-business!! involves selling through a website, which is **simple and low-cost**, allowing customers around the world to visit and place orders.
