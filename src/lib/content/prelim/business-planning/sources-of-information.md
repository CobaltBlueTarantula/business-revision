## Professional advisers

Professional advisers are more aware of changes within the **legal, economic and financial environment**. Business owners should use information from:

- !!Accountants:!! provide valuable advice on all **financial management issues** and **taxation obligations**. They have access to the **latest changes and requirements**.
- !!Solicitors:!! provide information concerning formation, structures, registration, contracts, leases, partneship agreements, patents and legislation. Up to date with any **changes to company law** and can advise the business owner.
- !!Bank managers:!! provide information and advice on **financial services** and **sources of finance**. Usually has wide range of **experience** and **resources** of the bank.
- [!!Management consultants:!!](../business-management/management-consultants) advises owner on management problems that they can't deal with, being more **objective and unbiased**.

## Government agencies

### Business connect

- Provides general and specialist **business advice** and **government information** to SMEs
- Promotes business **growth through innovation**, improving resilience and **boosting productivity**
- Supports digital readiness and regional business development

### Federal government

!!The Department of Industry, Science, Energy and Innovation:!! maintains a **website**, **contact centre** and a **national network** to **streamline access to information** and advice for businesses.

!!Australian Tax Office:!! the federal government's main **revenue collection agency**, which can provide information about **starting and running a business** as well as **taxation**.

!!Austrade:!! federal government's trade, investment and education promotion agency, which can help businesses **develop international markets** through **information, adivce and services**.

!!Digital business kits (DBK):!! provide **industry-specific information and resources** to help SMEs engage in the **digital econmy** and take advantage of **available opportunities**.

## Other sources

!!Chambers of commerce:!! **local associations of businesspeople**, centred on a **suburb or region**. They provide legal and financial help, taxation advice, legislation explanations and industrial relation information. These chambers also organise **training seminars** and arrange **industry conferences**.

!! Trade associations:!! offer specific **industry information and assistance**. Each association is made up of organisations that are in the same line of business, providing specific details about **product development** and **industry trends**.

!!Libraries:!! provide large amounts of **reference material** and subscribe to **trade and industry journals**.

!! Australian Bereau of Statistics (ABS):!! provides valuable data on **social, economic and demographic trends**, as well as specialised data on **business activities**. Having access to such information helps a business owner analyse changes in the **external business environment**.
