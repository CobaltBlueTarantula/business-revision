!!Organisational structure:!! the framework in which the business defines how **tasks are divided**, **resources are used** and **departments are coordinated**.

The five steps in the organising process:

1. Develop plans and establish goals
2. Determine activities
3. Group activities
4. Assign work tasks and delegate authority
5. Designa hierarchy of relationships

The benefits of a properly implemented organising process:

- Establishes a chain of command resulting in **orderly communication**
- Creates a **coordinated work environment** by outlining guidelines and responsibilities
- Provides a sense of **common purpose** when working to a common vision, goal or objective
- Organises resources in the **most efficient manner** for tasks to be performed

!!Resource allocation:!! the **efficient distribution of resources** to successfully meet the goals that have been established.

## Operations

- Required equipment and materials
- Suppliers of raw materials
- How much money needs to be allocated
- Necessary storage and delivery systems
- Employee technical expertise

## Marketing

- Integrated into all parts of the business
- Adequate resources for marketing plan
- Additional funds to accomplish marketing objectives

## Finance

- Most appropriate sources of finance
- Amount of equity must be handed over
- Government grants which do not have to be repaid

## Human resources

- Necessary workers and skills
- Attracting and selecting employees
- Motivating and retaining workers
- Training offered to workers
- Legal responsibilities towards employees
- Resolving arising disputes
- Access to the required types of workers
