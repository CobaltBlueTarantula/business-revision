Anyone thinking about starting a business should first gather some facts and figures to determine if the business would survive and generate profit.

## Feasibility study

!!Feasibility study:!! an assessment of the business idea's **potentioal for success**. This involves determining how practical the idea is and if it would **successfully translate to the market**.

A feasibility study might include:

- !!An assessment of the market:!! levels of demand, where potential customers are, competitive advantage, strengths and weaknesses.
- !!A consideration of the operations:!! describing the product, resources needed, location and legal requirements.
- !!An analysis of commercial feasibility:!! time until first sale, how price is determined, necessary finance, forecasting sales, costs, cashflow and profit.
- !!An appraisal of the owner's management ability:!! see [Personal qualities](personal-qualities).
- !!An understand of whether others have tried this idea:!! finding out whether this idea has been tried, and reasons for failure.

## Market research

!!Market research:!! the process of **systematically collecting**, recording and analysing information concerning a **specific marketing problem**.

The three steps of market research are:

1. **Determining** information needs
2. **Collecting** data from primary and secondary sources
3. **Analysing** and interpreting data

## Location

Local government zoning determines where some types of businesses can operate, **separating business activities from residential areas**.

!!Zoning:!! a means by which **local councils allocate land** for different uses, such as residential, commercial, recreational and industrial.

### Shopping centre complex

- Can be quite expensive
- Parking and transport access
- Undercover internal walkways

### Retail shopping strips

- Located near public transport
- High visibility for passing traffic
- Lack of parking

### Online presence

- Not limited by physical access
- Avoids expenses such as rent
- Can entirely replace a physical location

### Home-based businesses

- Quinnary services might not need a location
- Online ordering and payment provides more opportunities
- No expenditures on renting or purchasing premises
- Reduced risk
- Flexibility for owner
- Difficult to separate work and home life
- Isolation from other people
