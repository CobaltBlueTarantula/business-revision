!!Business plan:!! the business plan is a written summary and evaluation of all the **research and thinking** an SME owner has done in the **development of a business idea**. It is organised in such a way that all the factors necessary for the succes of the business (**operations, finance, marketing and human resources**) have been adequately covered. A detailed business plan greatly increases the **chance of success**.

## Contents

- A clear and concise statement of the **goals** of the business
- Well-developed plans for **achieving** these goals
- Reliable control standards for **measuring performance**

## Purposes

- All business plans should be presented in a **professional** manner because they may be read by bank managers, accountatnts, financial advisers, company directors, potential purchasers or interested partners.
- It is important the the plan **reflects the skills and abilities of the business owner**.
- A poorly presented plan will convey the impression that little thought has been given to the planning process, reflecting poorly on the **business owner and their capabilities**.
- A business plan will always be used throughout effective management and **adjusted** when changes occur.
