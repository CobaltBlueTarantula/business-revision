The requirement for the business whenever forecasting is used is to gain an accurate estimate of its **total revenue** in the **operational period**.
This provides a guide to its **expected profit levels**.

**Total cost:** all the costs incurred in the operation of the business, which are divided into **fixed costs** and **variable costs**.

## Break-even analysis

Forecasts the number of items to be sold in order to cover costs. The break even point is where the total revenu equals total costs.

**Break-even point = total fixed costs / unit price - variable cost per unit**

A break-even analysis helps by:

- providing a rough guide for the business
- showing if a venture will be profitable
- determining the profit or loss at different levels of sales

## Cashflow projections

Predicting **future cash inflows and outflows** for the business. Forecasts are needed to show how much money is needed, when it is needed, and where it will come from.
These projetions should highlight:

- Changes in the business's bank balance
- Timeframes (monthly, quarterly or yearly)
- Cash available to continue operations
- Periods of cash shortage or surplus
