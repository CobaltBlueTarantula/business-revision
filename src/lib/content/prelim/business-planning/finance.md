!!Working capital:!! **current assets - current liabilities**

!!Capital gain:!! the profit made for **selling shares for a higher price** than at which they were purchased.<br/>
!!Dividends:!! a company paying shareholders a **fraction of its net profit** per share owned.

## Debt finance

!!Debt finance:!! relates to the short-term and long-term **borrowing from external sources** by a business.

### Short-term

- !!Overdrafts:!! the bank allows a business to **overdraw their account** up to a limit for a specific time to **overcome a cash shortfall**.
- !!Commercial bills:!! short-term loans issued by **financial institutions** for larger amounts for a period between 30 and 180 days.
- !!Factoring:!! the **selling of accounts receivable** for a **discounted price** to a finance or factoring company.
- !!Accounts receivable:!! money owed to the business for goods or services it has sold but has **not yet been paid for**.

### Long-term

- !!Mortgage:!! a loan secured by the property of the borrower (business).
- !!Debentures:!! a bond issued by a company for a fixed rate of interest and for a fixed period of time.
- !!Unsecured note:!! a **loan from investors** for a set period of time. It is **not secured against the business's assets**.

## Equity finance

!!Equity finance:!! the funds contributed by the **business owner** to start and then expand the business. It **does not have to be repaid** unless the owner leaves the business, and there are **no interest payments** for the business.

- **Self-funding:** owner uses their personal finance to fund the business. There is a risk of losing all investment.
- **Family or friends:** quick and easy way to obtain extra finance. There is danger of affecting relationships.
- **Private investors:** investors may contribute funds to the business in return for a shre in its profit and equity. They are often willing to provide support and adivce to the owner, while receiving a return on their investment.
- **Shares:** companies can raise finance by selling shares in the business. Public companies can sell shares to the public and therefore access more finance. Known as **initial public offering (IPO)**.
- **Crowdfunding:** raising finance online through social media networks. Can be a quick way to raise finance and allows the owner to connect with potential customers, as well as receive feedback.
