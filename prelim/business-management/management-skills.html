Nature of Management

Skills of management

Interpersonal

Working around and through people to achive objectives.

It is also essential that managers can motivate and guide employees to reach their full potential. The result for the business is maximum labour input.

Communication

An effective manager can adjust their communication style to suit different types of people and employees.

Strategic thinking

Being able to create long-term plans requires seeing the business from a wholistic view. This includes understanding all individual functions and how they work together.

Decisions therefore need to be made to benefit the business as a whole.

Managers also need to consider how the business will maintain its competitive advantage in the long term.

Vision

Identifying the long-term goals management wants the business to achieve. Therefore, managers have to have a clear idea about where the business is headed and its future position in the market.

This vision will need to be communicated to all employees so they understand what decisions are being made and why.

Problem-solving

Problem solving refers to a range of activities directed at finding solutions. This first requires managers to identify the nature of the problem. Management must assess and choose the most appropriate strategy for the business.

The movement towards flatter organisational structures has increased worker participation in problem solving.

Decision-making

Generally, senior managers make decisions that involve the business as a whole and guide it towards achieving its goals. Middle managers are usually involved with more specific, functional decisions at the department level.

Flexibility

Managers need to remain flexible and adjust their plans to account for external changes.

Adaptability to change

Managers must anticipate changes may occur. Successful managers are proactive by anticipating changes in the business environment and making adjustments to plans, ensuring that the business can take advantage of opportunities and minimise threats.

Reconciling the conflicting interests of stakeholders

All stakeholders have their own reasons for having an interest in the business. Since people have different priorities, the hopes of one stakeholder may conflict with the desires of other groups.

Management will need to develop solutions to conflicts of interest. Involving employees in decision-making might also prevent conflict.
