Management Process

Operations

Goods and/or services

STM: simply transformed manufactured goods are those where the inputs have undergone few steps in processing to produce the output.
ETM: elaborately transformed manufactured goods are those where many inputs have undergone many changes and transformations to produce the output.

The production process

Job production

  • Outputs can be made on demands to suit what the customer requests
  • Highly flexible system
  • Low output
  • Usually less equipment and more use of skilled labour
  • Cost of unique outputs is quite high

Batch production

  • Outputs are made in groups or small amounts of the same output
  • Outputs are produced in batches, ranging from only a few to up to a few thousand
  • The business can simply add or remove batches based on demand

Flow production

  • Continuous flow of inputs that are transformed into outputs
  • Often used in assembly lines and large factories
  • Outputs look the same and have the same features
  • Usually less use of labour
  • Employees will need the skills to program and supervise equipment and technology

Quality management

Many businesses can use benchmarking to compare themselves with the rest of their industry and past performance.

Quality management: a strategy used by the business to ensure that the quality of the product meets business and cutomer expectations.

Quality control (checking)

Quality control involves establishing standards and measuring the outputs of a business against those standards. Physical inspections and checking of outputs can identify those outputs of poor quality.

Defect rate: the percentage of goods produced that are of an unacceptable inferior quality.

Quality assurance (preventing)

A more proactive form of control which involves establishing and using a set of prodecures and/or processes that will prevent products from having quality problems.

Total quality management (TQM)

An approach to quality control that relies on continuous improvements in all aspects of the business.

Quality circles: regular meetings of a group of employees from different section of the business to discuss issues arising in the workplace.
