Influences in Establishing an SME

Personal qualities

Qualifications

  • Business courses teach potential business owners to consider every aspect of the business idea and the importance of a business plan.
  • Formal education in business management should lower the failure rate of small businesses.

Skills

  • Entrepreneurs must ensure that they have the skills to transform a business opportunity into a real business.
  • Business owners need to have technical ability, including detailed knowledge of the product or service they are selling.
  • Skills come through experience, on-the-job training, familiarity with handling similar products or services, and formal training at a university, college or TAFE.
  • Industry experience gives a person technical knowledge of the product and its market, and connections with suppliers and potential customers.

Motivation

  • The main reason why individuals become busienss owners is their motivation to make a profit and improve their current financial position.
  • Many individuals have a strong desire to be independent, with the freedom to set hours of work, goals and approaches.
  • Some are unable to to obtain suitable work as an employee, thereforce they create their own business.
  • Some owners enjoy the challenge of starting a business from nothing.

Positive outcomes of motivation

  • Improved quality of life
  • Self-satisfaction
  • Increased wealth
  • Better lifestyle
  • Social recognition
  • Freedom to be your own boss

Entrepreneurship

An entrpreneur is someone who will take a calculated risk to turn an idea into a successful business operation. They will weigh up all the potential costs and compare them with the potential benefits of the new business venture.

Cultural background

  • Many business migrants may possess certain entrepreneurial skills, such as persistence, capacity for hard work and optimism due to their cultural heritage.
  • There may be advantages from dealing with suppliers of the same cultural background.
  • Shared experiences among migrants establishing businesses can lead to a more supportive business environment.

Gender

  • An increasing number of business owners are women.
  • Social acceptance and expectations have increased for women in the workplace.
  • Some data suggests that businesses with female owners have a lower failure rate.
  • Women are more likely to seek professional help sooner, preventing small problems from escalating.
  • Women often try to eliminate risk through research and planning, also choosing less risky alternatives.
  • Women are more commonly found in service industries, while men are likely to own construction, manufacturing and transport businesses.
