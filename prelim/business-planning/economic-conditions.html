Critical Issues in Business success and failure

Economic conditions

Economic conditions that promote business success

High economic conditions most likely mean that consumers are prepared to increase their spendings because consumer confidence is high. This means that consumers are certain their jobs are secure and incomes are expected to increase, giving them an optimistic outlook.

In periods of strong economic activity the economy will experience:

  • high levels of consumer spending means that businesses have a greater capacity to sell goods and services, therefore profits are maintained and improved. Businesses selling luxury items will benefit in these conditions due to a higher level of disposable income among consumers.
  • falling unemployment because businesses can afford to hire more employees when sales and profits are growing. The size of their workforce will also need to increase to account for the increase in demand.
  • increased production to meet high trends that provide opportunities for future increased sales and profit.

Economic conditions that lead to business failure

In an economic downturn, consumers are most likely not spending as much due to lower consumer confidence. This translated to reduced profits for businesses, eventually leading to lower production levels.

In periods of weak economic activity the economy will experience:

  • lower levels of consumer spending means that business have a reduced capacity to sell goods and services, leading to profit decline. Businesses selling necessities and budget goods will benefit in this economic condition.
  • rising unemployment as businessees cannot support current employee numbers when sales and profits are decreasing or negative. Cutting back production levels may reduce unsaleable inventory and employees may be made redundant.
  • decreased production in response to fall in consumer spending, resulting in profit decrease.
